The Challenge

Sound Familiar?

Global trade is full of opportunity — and complexity. These are the challenges we solve every day for distribution and logistics companies.

Customs Compliance Complexity

Tariff codes, trade agreements, regulatory changes — customs compliance is a moving target. One mistake can mean costly delays, penalties, or seized shipments. Staying compliant shouldn't require a law degree.

Supply Chain Visibility

When you can't see where your goods are in real time, every shipment is a guessing game. Lack of visibility leads to overstocking, stockouts, missed delivery windows, and unhappy customers.

Manual Shipment Tracking

Tracking shipments through emails, phone calls, and spreadsheets is slow, error-prone, and impossible to scale. As volume grows, so does the chaos — and the risk of lost or late deliveries.

Cross-Border Communication

Coordinating across time zones, languages, and regulations creates communication gaps that slow everything down. When your partner in Shanghai can't align with your warehouse in Dallas, shipments suffer.

Case Study

How a Family-Owned Distributor Expanded to 3 International Markets in 8 Months

The Challenge

A second-generation food distribution company had built a strong domestic operation but was losing bids to competitors who could serve international clients. The owner knew there was demand for their products in Mexico, Canada, and the Caribbean, but customs compliance, logistics coordination, and the technology gap felt insurmountable.

Our Solution

We partnered with them on a comprehensive expansion strategy. Phase one: customs compliance framework and trade route optimization for all three target markets. Phase two: a custom logistics platform integrating shipment tracking, automated customs documentation, and supplier communication. Phase three: on-the-ground operational setup and partner vetting.

The Results

  • First international shipment within 10 weeks of engagement
  • Active operations in 3 new markets within 8 months
  • International revenue accounting for 22% of total within year one
