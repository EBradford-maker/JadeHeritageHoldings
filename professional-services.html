Case Study

How a 30-Person Accounting Firm Eliminated 80% of Manual Data Entry

The Challenge

A growing CPA firm was losing senior associates to burnout. With 200+ clients, tax season meant 70-hour weeks — not because of complex accounting work, but because of manual data entry, document sorting, and report generation. The managing partner estimated that 40% of their time during peak season was spent on tasks that "a computer should be doing."

Our Solution

We built a custom AI-powered document processing pipeline that could ingest client documents (W-2s, 1099s, bank statements, receipts), extract relevant data using machine learning, categorize it automatically, and pre-populate their tax preparation software. We also built a client portal for secure document upload and real-time status updates, eliminating hundreds of "where's my return?" phone calls.

The Results

  • Manual data entry reduced by 80% across the firm
  • Average return preparation time dropped from 4.5 hours to 1.8 hours
  • Client satisfaction scores improved 34%
  • Staff overtime reduced by 60% during tax season
  • The firm onboarded 45 new clients without adding headcount

I've been in public accounting for 22 years, and this is the single biggest improvement to our practice I've ever seen. Our team went from dreading tax season to feeling in control. We're doing better work, our clients are happier, and for the first time in years, my senior associates aren't threatening to quit in April.
